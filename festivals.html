Festivals

Kolhapur, known for its vibrant culture and rich traditions, celebrates a variety of festivals throughout the year with immense enthusiasm. Here are some of the most notable ones:

Rankala Mahotsava

This annual five-day festival held near Rankala Lake celebrates the lively Kolhapuri way of life with cultural performances, music, art exhibitions, and competitions.

Jyotiba Yatra

A holy fair dedicated to Lord Jyotiba, this pilgrimage is one of the largest in Southern Maharashtra, lasting five weeks during the Hindu month of Chaitra. Devotees from various states flock to the Jyotiba Temple located on a hill near Panhala Fort.

Shree Mahalakshmi Kiranotsava

A unique event celebrated twice a year (January 31st to February 2nd and November 9th to 11th) where the setting sun's rays fall directly on the idol of Goddess Mahalaxmi inside the temple, a testament to the temple's architectural brilliance.
